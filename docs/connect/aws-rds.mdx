---
title: Connect AWS RDS
description: Connect Dora to an Amazon RDS or Aurora database (PostgreSQL or MySQL).
---

Amazon RDS and Aurora host PostgreSQL and MySQL. RDS gives you an endpoint and
port rather than a ready-made URL, so you usually assemble the connection string
yourself.

## Build the connection string

1. In the AWS Console open **RDS → Databases** and select your instance.
2. From the **Connectivity & security** tab copy the **Endpoint** and **Port**.
3. Assemble a URL with your master username, password, and database name:

   ```text
   postgresql://<user>:<password>@<endpoint>.rds.amazonaws.com:5432/<db>
   ```

   For MySQL use `mysql://` and port `3306`.

4. In Dora press `Mod+Shift+N`, toggle **Use connection string**, paste the URL,
   **Test connection**, and **Save**.

<Callout type='warn'>Your RDS instance must be reachable from your machine: it needs **Public access = Yes** (or a VPN / bastion), and its **security group** must allow inbound traffic from your IP on the database port. To reach a private instance through a bastion, use Dora's [SSH tunnel](/docs/connect#ssh-tunnels).</Callout>

Enable the **SSL** toggle if your instance enforces TLS (`rds.force_ssl`). You
may also need the Amazon RDS CA certificate depending on your `sslmode`.
